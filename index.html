Minnesota ranked the healthiest state in the U.S, West Virginia last

By Asad Jung

Dec. 17 2023

Minnesota had the lowest death rate out of any state in the U.S. in 2017, according to data published by the U.S. Department of Health and Human services. The death rate in Minnesota was 656.4, while the national average was 739.1. Minnesota also had the lowest rate of deaths related to heart disease: the national average was 165 while Minnesotaâ€™s was 119. Heart disease is the number one killer in the United States, according to the same data. Other states with low death rates were New York, Connecticut, California, and Hawaii.

The state with the highest death rate was West Virginia, at 957. The number one killer in West Virginia was heart disease, with cancer as a close runner up. In 2008, the Associated Press named Huntington, West Virginia as the unhealthiest place in America, according to health survey data from the CDC. It also has the highest rate of obesity in the United States. Other states with higher than average death rates were Mississippi, Kentucky, Alabama, and Oklahoma.
